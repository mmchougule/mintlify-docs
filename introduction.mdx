b402 is a TypeScript SDK for composable confidential DeFi on Solana. This page explains what b402 does, what is deployed on mainnet today, and how its components fit together. If you want to jump straight to code, head to the [Quickstart](/quickstart).

## What b402 does

b402 gives your application a shielded balance that participates in DeFi without exposing your wallet as the executing party. You deposit tokens once â€” that shield transaction links your wallet to an amount, and that is the last time your address appears. Every action after that (swap, lend, withdraw) is signed by the b402 relayer, not you.

- **Shield** USDC or wSOL from your wallet into the b402 pool.
- **Swap privately** through Jupiter routing â€” the on-chain swap transaction contains the relayer's pubkey, the adapter program, and the Jupiter route, but not your wallet.
- **Lend on Kamino** from your shielded balance into any Kamino reserve. Each viewing key gets its own Kamino `Obligation`, so positions are unlinkable across users.
- **Unshield to any address** â€” there is no on-chain edge connecting the withdrawal to the original deposit.

<Note>
  Only `shield` requires your wallet signature. All other operations â€” `swap`, `lend`, `redeem`, `unshield` â€” are submitted by the hosted relayer. The relayer pays gas on your behalf.
</Note>

## Architecture

b402 has four main components.

**Pool program** â€” the on-chain UTXO pool. It maintains a Merkle commitment tree and a nullifier set. Every shield creates a new commitment leaf. Every spend proves a nullifier has not been seen before.

**Verifier programs** â€” two Groth16 verifiers (one for plain transfers, one for adapter-bound swaps and lending). They are called by the pool program via CPI and verify the zero-knowledge proof before any state change is applied.

**Adapters** â€” registered Anchor programs that receive a CPI from the pool. Jupiter and Kamino adapters are live. Adding a new protocol is one Anchor crate (~200â€“300 lines) implementing the `execute(action_payload)` ABI plus a registry entry â€” no circuit recut or ceremony required.

**Relayer** â€” a hosted Cloud Run service that submits unshield, swap, lend, and redeem transactions on behalf of users. The relayer's pubkey appears on-chain as the fee payer; the depositor's wallet does not. The SDK defaults to the hosted relayer automatically.

<Info>
  The entire shielded-swap or shielded-deposit flow is one instruction in one versioned transaction with one signature. An Address Lookup Table compresses every common account (pool program, both verifiers, USDC and wSOL mints, system and token programs, b402 vaults) from 32-byte inline pubkeys to 1-byte ALT indexes.
</Info>
